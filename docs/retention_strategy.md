# Retention Strategy Playbook

## Purpose

The model should not stop at prediction. It should drive actions that reduce churn risk and improve renewal conversion.

## Risk segments and actions

### `critical_risk`

Signals:

- very high churn probability
- low usage and weak relationship health
- unresolved support or billing issues

Actions:

- executive escalation within 48 hours
- account recovery plan owned by CSM + account executive
- issue-resolution war room for product/support blockers
- renewal negotiation with targeted commercial flexibility only if root cause is understood

### `high_risk`

Signals:

- elevated churn probability
- declining adoption or engagement
- poor sponsor coverage or weak success cadence

Actions:

- schedule a value review
- rebuild success plan around measurable business outcomes
- targeted enablement for underused features
- sponsor mapping and relationship expansion

### `watchlist`

Signals:

- moderate churn probability
- mixed product health
- early warning on support, adoption, or payment behavior

Actions:

- increase monitoring frequency
- run adoption campaigns
- address small support and billing friction before renewal pressure builds

### `healthy`

Signals:

- low churn probability
- stable usage and relationship metrics

Actions:

- maintain regular success motion
- validate renewal timeline
- identify advocacy opportunities

### `expansion_ready`

Signals:

- high renewal probability
- strong adoption and business value realization

Actions:

- position upsell/cross-sell plays
- secure early renewal
- request case study, referral, or reference participation

## KPI tracking after deployment

- churn rate by predicted risk band
- renewal rate by predicted risk band
- save rate after intervention
- uplift by action type
- precision / recall for high-risk accounts
- calibration of predicted probabilities versus actual outcomes

## Practical notes

- Risk alone is not enough; include account value when prioritizing.
- Separate "cannot renew" from "won't renew" drivers when possible.
- Review recommendations monthly to avoid stale playbooks.
